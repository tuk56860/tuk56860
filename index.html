GUS 3062: Fundamentals of Geographic Information Systems

This course teaches the theory and practical use of Geographic Information Systems (GIS). Major components of the course include vector and raster spatial data models and operations, including vector overlay and raster map algebra. At the end of the course students are expected to have an understanding of elementary GIS theory, working knowledge of a GIS software package, and the ability to develop GIS-based solutions to geographic modeling and analysis tasks. !

Recent Projects

Covid-19 Case Fatality Rates

This map displays which US states had higher black populations among those with relatively high case fatality rates. The tools used for this were "select by attribute" and the skills were data calculation and mapping. I used Covid-19 data and selected the states where case fatality was greater than 2. Using the new table, I selected the states where the black population was greater than 1200000. I mapped the data, and the states that are outlined in blue display the US states that had higher black populations among those with relatively high case fatality rates.
Visual Effects of Various CRS Projections

The purpose of this assignment was to discuss and compare four different world maps that each used a different CRS in terms of the purpose, applications, cartographic properties, and limitations of each projection. One skill utilized was define projection to assign spatial context to data with unknown coordinate information. An additional skill learned was finding the coordinate system of a layer, which aids in understanding the spatial properties of a dataset and cartographic presentation. For this assignment, I created four graphs, each with a different CRS and created a multi-frame layout to display all the maps at once.

Spatial Distribution of the Hispanic Population in the US by State

The objective of this assignment was to create choropleth and proportional symbol maps using Census data at the national scale. The skills used for this assignment was symbology and data field identification. Symbology was utilized to visually represent population patterns in the US. Data field identification was used to specify which aspects of the dataset were relevent for mapping and analysis. The "graduated colors" feature of the symbology tool was used to map the percentages of of Hispanic accross US states.

2020 PA Election Results

For this assignment, the objective was to identify which counties in PA voted for Biden and which ones voted for Trump in the 2020 election. The skills utilized were Join and Symbology. The join feature was used to merge PA county and County voting datasets by county name. Then, the "histogram" feature of the choropleth map tool was used to adjust the thresholds for the percentage of votes. Then, colors were added to each value depending on whether Trump or Biden has the majority of votes.
